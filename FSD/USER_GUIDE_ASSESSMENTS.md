# Assessment User Guide (Candidates)

Quick guide for taking assessments as a candidate.

---

## Before you start

- Use a supported browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge, Safari).
- For **voice** and **video** sections: allow microphone and camera; use a quiet place and good lighting.
- For **code** sections: you’ll write and run code in the browser; no local IDE required.
- You can take breaks between sections; progress is saved when you complete or continue each section.

---

## Taking an assessment

1. **Open the assessment**  
   Use the link from your invitation (email or assignment). You may need to sign in.

2. **Read the instructions**  
   The first screen shows duration, number of sections, and rules. Click **I'm Ready, Start** when you’re ready.

3. **Work through each section**  
   - You’ll see one section at a time (e.g. “Leadership – Multiple Choice”, “Communication – Voice Interview”).
   - Click **Start section** to begin. A timer may be shown for that section.
   - Complete all questions or tasks in the section, then click **Continue** or **Submit section** to move on.
   - If time runs out, the section is submitted automatically.

4. **Section types**
   - **Multiple choice / Situational / Essay:** Answer on screen; use **Next** or **Submit** as shown.
   - **Voice interview:** You’ll hear questions and record your answers; follow the on-screen prompts.
   - **Video interview:** Record a short video per question; you may have limited retakes.
   - **Code challenge:** Write code in the editor, run tests, then submit your solution.
   - **Adaptive questions:** Same as multiple choice; difficulty adjusts as you answer.
   - **Panel interview:** This section is scheduled separately. You’ll see a short explanation and can optionally **Schedule** a date/time (and meeting link). When done, click **Continue to next section**.

5. **Finish**  
   After the last section you’ll be taken to a completion or results page (depending on settings).

---

## Tips

- Don’t refresh or close the tab during a section; you might lose unsaved answers.
- For voice/video, speak clearly and stay within the time limit per question.
- For code, run tests before submitting to catch basic errors.
- If something fails (e.g. save error), you can often still **Continue**; your progress may be partial.

---

## Need help?

Contact your assessor or the person who sent you the assessment link if you have access or technical issues.
